Notes for build:

1. Cloudflare + GitHub (The Ultimate Setup)
Yes, this is a brilliant idea. What you are describing is called "Continuous Deployment," and it is the industry standard.

How it works: You host the code on a free GitHub repository. You connect that repo to Cloudflare Pages (which is 100% free). Every time you push an update to GitHub, Cloudflare automatically rebuilds the live website in about 30 seconds.

Why it’s perfect: It costs zero dollars, includes a free SSL certificate (the little padlock icon so the site is secure), and Cloudflare's servers are incredibly fast, which Google loves for SEO.

2. The CTA Strategy (Don't Cause "Analysis Paralysis")
While having a phone number, email, and form seems like it gives the user options, giving them too many equal choices actually lowers conversions.

Primary CTA (The Big Button): "Get a Free Quote" (This links to a form). Forms are best because you can force the client to give you exactly what you need (Name, Address, Type of Roof, etc.) before you ever speak to them.

Secondary CTA (Top Right Corner): A clickable Phone Number. Some older folks or people with emergencies just want to call. Make it easy to find, but don't make it the main button.

What to drop: The raw email address. Putting contact@cedarshingle.com on the site just invites automated spam bots to scrape it. Force them through the form.
